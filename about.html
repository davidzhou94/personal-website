More About Me

Well, internet stranger, it seems you want to know more about me. If you were more interested in my professional or academic history, you can click here for my CV, here for my LinkedIn or here for my GitHub. The content on this page is more about my interests and personal life.

Biography

My life began when I was born in 1994. My parents and I lived in the southern city of Shenzhen before we moved to Canada in 2000. In 2006 my mom and I became Canadian citizens and that day remains one of my happiest memories. Before high school, I attended three elementary schools, the last of which was because I qualified for the gifted program in my school board. I continued in the gifted program at Markham District High School from which I graduated in 2012. While at MDHS, I was fortunate to have had teachers who cared about the success of their students. Such was their dedication that my school had an unparalleled extra-curricular program (which I only realized to be unique after comparing with peers in university). On many school days I wouldn't go home until hours after school ended because I was staying for Improv, Reach for the Top (trivia), LEAD (leadership), Debate, theatre, Computer Science (club), or the countless events of clubs my friends were in. When university began, I decided it was time for a break.

I started at McGill University in 2012 with the intention of studying Economics and Mathematics as a first step towards a senior technical analyst position in a major international organization like the IMF or the UNDP. By the end of my first semester I realized that math is hard. My experience in Calculus led me to believe that I am fairly average relative to my peers. After a series of fleeting program decisions, I settled on Computer Science (focusing on Software Engineering) and Economics. It seemed natural to be "back" in Computer Science. I had taken two courses in high school and some programming classes outside of school. The good career prospects were a motivating factor but I mostly made my decision because Computer Science is something I have always liked. It was the problem-solving aspect that I found particularly enjoyable, and by problem solving I really mean Googling StackOverflow.

In my final year at school, I was lucky to have found a job early in the school semester so I could focus on my courses. That year, like many computer science students at McGill, I chose to take a couple "introductory" classes at the graduate level. I took a course in distributed systems and another in compiler design, where my team won a prize for best compiler. Those two courses were hands-down the best courses I had taken at McGill. After graduating in June 2016, I did some travelling with friends and family before starting at Amazon Robotics in August 2016 where I am working now.
